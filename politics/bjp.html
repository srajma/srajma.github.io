1. scale

Also published on Twitter

India’s longest-standing weakness has been inability to scale. We pioneered many things, only to keep them as white elephants instead of mass-produce and universalize them.

India under Modi has decisively overcome this limitation. 5G, rail electrification, UPI rollout, infrastructure spree, heck even toilets are examples of this.

Absolutely monumental achievement.

Whatever little good Nehru or the British Raj did (dwarfed by their evils), were for a few thousand. Whatever Modi did was for 1.4 billion. By virtue of scale alone, Modi’s achievements dwarf Nehru’s by literally a million.

2. scale 2

Also published on Twitter

The best way to understand Nehruvian and British Raj governance is as a White Elephant Economy.

In a country of continental proportions, Nehru/British built like you would for a little city-state.

4 universities whose total intake is less than a single Ivy League, and marked the job as done. Made one Rajdhani train service and they have the audacity to ask “what’s special about Vande Bhārat, we always had Rajdhani!”

No vision to scale for the masses. Seeing infrastructure as a monument to kang on rather than an economic instrument that benefits as many people as possible.

This is also why Indians have the illusion today that their country is “overpopulated” despite having a lower population density than England. Because in a country of continental proportions, Congress/British built like you would for a tiny city-state.

Perhaps Modi’s greatest achievement is in overcoming this long-standing limitation. 5G, rail electrification, UPI rollout, infrastructure spree, heck even toilets are examples of this. For the first time, India is able to do things at scale.

By contrast BJP has almost a child-like faith in the inherent goodness of Indians.

In 2024 they placed candidates ignoring caste equations, promised no freebies, and Modi attacked anti-capitalist rhetoric head-on saying “yes I have pride when our companies succeed”.

People think that Congress’s chaiwala jibes in 2014 were an obvious mistake … not really. It failed then, but they are doing it again with crass memes about Modi’s accent and English, and with more success.

It’s a type of rhetoric that works on a certain type of insecure, status-conscious and Western validation-seeking person. And the rest of their rhetoric to zero-sum, crab-mentality thinkers. And this is what Congress bets on Indians being.

While the BJP bets on the best in Indians: to their aspirations, to their united Hindu identity without regard for random parochialisms.

May the idealists win, and may the cynics perish.
